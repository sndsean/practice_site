Greens Case Training

Prepping Greens

  • Trim butt, remove excess/bad outer leaves- the minimum you can to make it look nice
  • Wrap in a band if applicable. Blue "garden fresh" bands are available to wrap anything that does not usually need a band.
  • Drop in tepid (60 - 100 degree) water to hydrate for approx. 15 minutes
  • Drain for a second before turning upside down (or in a way to allow water to drain from the product) in a greens crate

Culling Flowchart

  • Is product turning yellow/brown/black/slimy?
    • Yes
      • Can leaves be removed to restore quality?
        • Yes- Put in a banana box to rework
          • Replace with fresh product
        • No- Trash
    • No
      • Is product wilting?
        • Yes
          • Put in banana box to rework
            • Replace with fresh product
        • No- Trim butt and keep on shelf

Having prepped backstock ready to go

Always have 1 crate of each prepped: red leaf, green leaf, and romaine. In the 4th basket, have a full display's worth (i.e. the amount you would normally build to) prepped and ready to go- 3 leeks, 2 escarole, 2 chicory, 4 kale, 4 collards, etc. The only exception would be bulk carrots, cabbages, broccoli, napa, herbs. Herbs, chards, and spinaches don't like being soaked for too long as they'll get waterlogged.

Greens case maintenance

  • Every day- wipe down shelves and price strips
  • Every week- pour hot water down drains and well and cabbage/broccoli tubs
  • Every quarter- complete greens case teardown to the fans
